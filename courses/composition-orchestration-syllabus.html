Composition/Orchestration Syllabus

Course Description

This course is designed to allow beginning and intermediate composers and orchestrators to broaden their understanding of various compositional techniques and styles through lessons centered around the needs and interests of members. Members and directors decide on lesson topics together and will also decide on a work of music to score read for each session.

Each session will proceed with the following activities, which may be adjusted or omitted to meet time constraints:

  • Score Reading
  • Composition/Orchestration Video Lesson
  • Topical Composition/Orchestration Lesson

Each member may send a score to a director for feedback during the course.

Instructions

  • Communicate and discuss workable dates and times for each session with other members in the group chat
  • Join the meeting using the link on a calendar event

Expectations

  • Prepare for each session by accessing the course website and clicking on the correct session day.
  • Participate actively in activities! These sessions are much more fun when they become discussion-based.
  • Put forth your best effort. Some days will be easy and some will be hard, but we want to see what you can do!
  • If you need extra time before the director moves on to a new activity or new section of a score, please ask!

Recommendations

  • It takes time and sustained effort to grow as a composer or orchestrator. Set aside a bit of time every day to write a bit of music, even if it's just a handful of minutes. If that doesn't work, try setting aside time on weekends.
  • On some days, you may feel very inspired and write a lot. On others, you might hesitate to commit anything to the page. That's okay! It's more important that you make a consistent effort even if your output isnâ€™t always consistent.
  • Listening to music is integral to writing or orchestrating music, so find the time to listen to it. Even if it just means that you listen while doing chores or browsing the web, it will still help. If you want to maximize your benefits, set some time aside to focus on listening to music exclusively, and grab a score of the music to read along!

Rules

  1. Be civil and respectful at all times, to all members.
  2. Self-promotion is not allowed.
  3. Spamming in chats or disrupting sessions is prohibited.
  4. No NSFW content is allowed during sessions or in scores sent in for feedback.
  5. Plagiarizing music, regardless if the source material is complete or not, is strictly prohibited.
  6. Do not reveal personal information.
  7. Excessive swearing is prohibited.
  8. Messages and speech must be in English unless specifically referencing a work that is in another language.

This list of rules is not exhaustive and may be changed over time. Real-life caution should be exercised.

Members who violate the above rules may be subject to the following consequences:

  • Losing access to courses directed by The Composers Foundry
  • Losing membership to The Composers Foundry (temporarily or permanently)
